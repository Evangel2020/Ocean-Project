OUR OCEAN

What's The Future Of The Ocean?

Do you know, that the Oceans cover more than two thirds of Earth’s surface. They are home to millions of species, provide a key source of protein to people on every continent, and play an enormous role in regulating our planet’s climate, water cycle and more. They also are facing tremendous disruption from human action, from altered temperature and circulation to overfishing to acidification to plastic pollution, and the value of a plastic garbage truck is thrown into the sea every minute. A sailor named Dame Ellen MacArthur, who sailed more oceans than anyone else. She warned that if industries and people do not stop polluting the ocean, there will be more plastic waste in the sea than fish by 2050. About seven million tonnes of waste is dumped into the world's seas and oceans each year. Car tires, bottles, plastic bags, fishing nets are among the most common waste. It is estimated that this affects will kill more than 600 species of marine fauna, from whales to turtles, and even birds, and that 100,000 marine mammals die every year from garbage!

Why Should We Care About The Ocean ?

The ocean offers us countless benefits. Here are seven reason why we should care.

The Air We Breathe: The ocean produces over half of the world's oxygen and absorbs 50 times more carbon dioxide than our atmosphere.

Climate Regulation: The ocean is responsible to transport heat from the equator to the poles, regulating our climate and weather patterns.

Transportation: Around 90% of world trade is carried by the international shipping industry.

Recreation: From fishing to boating to kayaking to surfing and whale watching, the ocean offers us with many unique activities.

Economic benefits: In 2014, the ocean economy, which includes six economic sectors that depend on the ocean and Great Lakes, contributed more than $352 billion to the U.S. GDP and supported 3.1 million jobs.

Food: The ocean provides more than just seafood; ingredients from the sea are found in surprising foods such as peanut butter, soymilk and Salt.

Medicine: Many medicinal products come from the ocean, including ingredients that help fight against cancer, arthritis, Alzheimer's disease, and heart disease.
